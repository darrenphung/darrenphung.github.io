VR Basketball

Designing for Virtual Reality 2018

Team: Darren Phung / John Antonios / Jaeho Choi

Role: 3D Modelling, Visual Design, UX Research

This project was made to create a VR Basketball game for those that are unable to play due to disability, injury or sickness. This allows the player to immerse themselves in the same environment where they are not able to, due to their circumstances.

The Design Brief

How do we design technological solutions that improve the quality of our everyday life and make us happier? Is it possible to enhance happiness through technology solutions? Can a VR solution present an opportunity for personal growth and help us become the person we want to be? These are some of the questions we have to think about in the context of our design project.

User Group

Our user group should satisfy this criteria:



  • Young adult aged 18 - 24
  • Interested in Basketball, but any other sport should suffice
  • May have or had a physical injury on their lowerbody, i.e. legs, ankle, hip, etc., that made them unable to play
  • If they were in rehab from injury or during leisure time/unable to play basketball.

Design Precedence

Our design brief states that VR can be utilised to assist young adults to train and/or rehabilitate their basketball techniques from physical injuries on the bottom half of their body causing them to be immobilised.

There are several case studies that reveal how virtual reality can assist people to regain their performance to some extent through simulation.

VR was used for balance training for cycling training, where the patients postural balance control as had a significant improvement after a period of cycling training in the virtual (2015)

Continuously, there have been numerous VR-utilised practises such as rehabilitation programs for recovering kinetic ability of damaged lower limbs through road cycling program, and exoskeletal rehabilitation program.
